  • Type 1 Diabetes

    Type 1 is known as juvenile diabetes, where the pancreas cannot produce enough insulin (often due to death of beta/islet cells by the body's immune system). There is currently no cure.

  • Type 2 Diabetes

    Type 2 is generally characterized as an adult-onset disease where the body develops resistance to insulin or cannot produce enough insulin, so that blood sugar levels remain increased in the body.

  • Type 3 Diabetes

    Type 3 is caused by Alzheimer's disease, wherein neurons in the brain become unable to respond to insulin.

  • LADA Diabetes

    This is a type of diabetes 1 that shares some characteristics with type 2. LADA develops into adulthood.

  • MODY Diabetes

    This form of diabetes is more likely to be inherited genetically and stands for Maturity Onset Diabetes of the Young.

  • Gestational Diabetes

    This form of diabetes only occurs in certain pregnancies due to hyperglycemia. This is due to hormonal changes and the growth demands of the fetus - however, this form of diabetes is reversible after pregnancy.

  • Steroid Induced Diabetes

    Steroid induced diabetes is caused by increased levels of corticosteroids, which mimic cortisol - the hormone that regulates bodily response to increased glucose levels. Thus, corticosteroids increase insulin resistance.

  • Diabetes Insipidus

    This special case of diabetes is not related to blood sugar, but shares some symptoms. DI is due to excessive urination, caused by hormone imbalance in the kidneys.
